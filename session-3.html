Welcome to Session 3 — Street Food: Vocabulary and Grammar.

In this lesson, you'll learn food vocabulary: noodles, samosa, kebab, falafel, taco, burger, pho, koshari, kunafa, gyro.

You'll also learn food adjectives: delicious, tasty, crispy, spicy, bland, greasy, tender, savory, juicy, salty, sweet, sour.

For grammar, we study some and any:

Some — used in positive sentences: "I want some rice." "There are some vegetables."

Any — used in negative sentences and questions: "There isn't any rice." "Are there any samosas?"

We also review a/an for singular countable nouns: "a burger", "an egg".

And how much (uncountable) vs how many (countable): "How much rice?" vs "How many burgers?"

📝 Show Subtitle / Transcript

The Psychology of Street Food Negotiation.

When you buy street food, you often need to communicate with the vendor. Here are useful phrases:

Ordering: "Can I have some falafel, please?" "I'd like a kebab." "How much is it?"

Asking about ingredients: "Is there any cheese in this?" "Does it have any spice?" "What's in the samosa?"

Describing what you want: "I want something spicy." "Do you have anything sweet?" "I'd like it without onions."

Negotiating: "Is that the best price?" "Can I get two for less?" "That's a bit expensive."

Complimenting: "This is delicious!" "The falafel is really crispy!" "It's the best koshari I've ever had!"

Practice these phrases — you'll need them when traveling!

📝 Step 3: Grammar

Countable vs Uncountable Nouns

Countable: things you can count — 1 taco, 2 tacos, 3 eggs

Uncountable: things you can't count — rice, chicken (as food), bread, water

Trick: Some words change! One chicken = the animal (countable). Chicken on your plate = food (uncountable). Same with lamb.

How to count uncountable things? Put them in a container: a bottle of water, a bag of rice, a can of soda, a carton of milk.

Articles: a / an / the / zero article

ArticleRuleExample
aONE thing (consonant sound), first mentionI had a falafel sandwich
anONE thing (vowel sound), first mentionShe bought an orange
theSPECIFIC thing (both know which one)The tacos we ate were tasty
zeroGeneral / plural / uncountable (not specific)We love eating — street food

Some & Any

CountableUncountable
✅ PositiveThere are some tacos.There is some rice.
❌ NegativeThere aren't any tacos.There isn't any rice.
❓ QuestionAre there any tacos?Is there any rice?
Exception: Use some in questions when offering: "Would you like some tea?" or "Do you need some chicken?"

How much / How many / Too much / Too many / Enough

WordUse WithExample
How muchUncountableHow much sugar is there?
How manyCountableHow many eggs are left?
Too muchUncountable (excessive)There's too much salt!
Too manyCountable (excessive)There are too many onions.
EnoughBoth (sufficient)We have enough food. / There are enough plates.

🌍 10 Best Street Foods Around the World

Hey everyone, welcome back to World Insights by Heron. If you're a foodie like us, you'll love today's video. We're exploring the 10 best street foods around the world, from sizzling skewers in Istanbul to spicy bites in India.

At number 10, we kick things off with a savory-filled classic from Pakistan: the Samosa. These triangle pockets of goodness are stuffed with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. They're deep-fried to golden perfection and served with a variety of chutneys.

Next up at number nine, we have Vada Pav from India. Often dubbed as Mumbai's very own burger, this dish features a crispy potato fritter called Vada, which is stuffed in a pav bread roll, topped with chutneys and fried green chillies.

At number eight, we're heading to Mexico for the iconic Taco. These street foods come in many flavors and with fresh toppings like cilantro, onion, and a splash of lime. Over 10 billion tacos are consumed annually in Mexico.

At number seven, we've got the Doner Kebab from Turkey. The tender seasoned meat, usually lamb or chicken, is roasted on a vertical rotisserie and served in a pita or flatbread with a variety of fresh toppings.

Coming in at number six is Pho, Vietnam's national dish. This fragrant soup features rice noodles, aromatic herbs, and a rich broth, often with slices of beef or chicken.

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay — At the Food Market

Click each role card to see the guiding points and an example conversation. Practice with a partner or read aloud!

🧑‍🍳 Role 1: Street Food Vendor

  • Offer a specific food to the customer
  • Ask how much/how many the customer wants
  • Offer options (vegetarian, spicy, etc.)
  • Ask if they want extra items
  • Offer a sample and ask how many they want to buy
  • Suggest the most popular item from the menu
Vendor: Hello! Would you like to try some falafel? It's our most popular item!
Customer: Yes, it smells delicious! How much is it?
Vendor: One sandwich is 30 pounds. How many would you like?
Customer: I'll take two, please. Do you have any spicy sauce?
Vendor: Yes! We have some hot chili sauce. Would you like to try a sample first?
Customer: Sure! Oh, that's really spicy but tasty! Can I also get some fries with that?
Vendor: Of course! Here you go. Enjoy your meal!

❤️ Role 2: Food Lover

  • Talk about what food you want to try
  • Ask for the name of a specific food and describe its flavors
  • Ask how much something costs or how many you can get
  • Say if you like or don't like some food
  • Ask if there are any vegetarian or special options
  • Mention how many servings you want to order
Food Lover: Excuse me, what are those round things? They smell amazing!
Vendor: Those are samosas! They're filled with some spiced potatoes and peas.
Food Lover: Mmm, mouth-watering! Are there any vegetarian options?
Vendor: Yes, the potato samosa is vegetarian. We also have some cheese rolls.
Food Lover: How much are the cheese rolls?
Vendor: 25 pounds each.
Food Lover: I'll try two samosas and a cheese roll. I love trying new food. I'm starving!

💰 Role 3: Budget-Careful Shopper

  • Ask about the price (use how much)
  • Ask if there are any cheaper options or discounts
  • Talk about how many portions you'd like
  • Ask if any items are on sale or if they can make it smaller
  • Request some extra food like toppings or sides
Shopper: How much is a plate of noodles?
Vendor: A full plate is 45 pounds.
Shopper: That's too much for me. Are there any cheaper options?
Vendor: We have a small plate for 25 pounds.
Shopper: That sounds better. How many pieces of chicken come with it?
Vendor: Three pieces. Would you like some extra vegetables? They're free!
Shopper: Yes, please! Can I also get some bread on the side?
Vendor: Sure, here's a piece of fresh bread. Enjoy!

📝 Role 4: Food Critic

  • Ask for a description of the food (flavor, ingredients)
  • Talk about the qualities of the food (spicy, sweet, etc.)
  • Ask if the food is good for certain diets
  • Ask if any changes can be made (less spicy, add something)
  • Ask about how much/how many ingredients are used
Critic: Good afternoon. Can you describe this dish for me? What's in it?
Vendor: It's a traditional doner kebab with some lamb, fresh vegetables, and our special sauce.
Critic: Interesting. Is it spicy?
Vendor: It's a little spicy, yes. We use some chili flakes.
Critic: Can you make it less spicy? I don't want too much chili.
Vendor: Of course! We can leave out the chili.
Critic: The meat is very tender and juicy. How many spices do you use?
Vendor: We use seven different spices! It's our secret recipe.
Critic: Impressive. The bread is soft and fluffy too. I'd rate this four out of five stars!
