Describing Friends with This & That

Grammar + vocabulary combined naturally

📝 Show Subtitle / Transcript

Welcome to Session 4 — Describing Friends with This, That, These, and Those.

We use this and these for things close to us:

"This is my best friend." (one person, close)

"These are my classmates." (many people, close)

We use that and those for things far from us:

"That is my neighbor." (one person, far)

"Those are the new students." (many people, far)

Now let's combine with friendship vocabulary:

"This friend is very kind."

"That moment was special."

"These traits make a good friend."

"Those friends are very loyal."

📝 Step 3: Grammar — This, That, These, Those

The Rule (Simple!)

We use these words to point at people or things. Two questions decide which word:

1. Is it near you or far?   2. Is it one or many?

👈 Near👉 Far
One (singular)thisthat
Many (plural)thesethose

Examples with Friends

This is my best friend. (one person, next to me)

That is her classmate over there. (one person, far away)

These are my close friends. (group, near me)

Those are their teammates. (group, far away)

Also before nouns: "I enjoy spending time with this friend." / "Those people are very kind."

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay — Talking About Friends

Click each role card to see the guiding points and example conversation. Practice with a partner!

🙋 Role 1: The Introducer

Introduce your best friend to someone new. Describe their qualities.

  • Introduce your friend by name using "this" or "that"
  • Describe at least 3 personality traits
  • Give a specific example of when they showed each trait
  • Explain why this person is your best friend
You: Hey! This is my best friend, Nour. She's really kind and supportive.
New person: Nice to meet you! What makes her so special?
You: Well, she's very honest. She always tells me the truth in a kind way. Last week, I was stressed about an exam, and she said "You can do it!" That really helped me.
New person: She sounds amazing!
You: She is! These are the qualities that make her my best friend. I'm really grateful for her.

💚 Role 2: The Grateful Friend

Thank a friend for something they did for you.

  • Tell your friend what they did that helped you
  • Use "this/that/these/those" to refer to moments
  • Describe how their help made you feel
  • Use at least 2 personality traits
You: I want to say thank you for helping me with my project last week.
Friend: Of course! What are friends for?
You: You're so helpful and encouraging. That moment when you stayed late to help me really meant a lot.
Friend: I'm glad I could help!
You: Those words you said — "You can do it!" — really lifted my spirit. You're the most loyal friend I have.

👯 Role 3: The Comparer

Describe two friends and compare their qualities.

  • Introduce two friends using "this" and "that"
  • Describe different traits of each friend
  • Give examples of how they show those traits
  • Say what you appreciate about both
You: This is my friend Sara, and that is my friend Mona over there.
Listener: How are they different?
You: Sara is very funny. She always makes me laugh. Mona is more empathetic — she understands my feelings and gives me advice.
Listener: They sound like great friends!
You: These two are very different, but I love them both. Those are the kind of friends everyone needs!

💭 Role 4: The Friendship Advisor

Give advice to someone about being a good friend.

  • Explain what qualities a good friend should have
  • Use "these" to list important traits
  • Give real examples of good and bad friend behavior
  • Use at least 4 vocabulary words from the session
Person: I want to be a better friend. What should I do?
You: These are the most important things: be honest, be supportive, and always listen.
Person: Can you give me an example?
You: Sure! If your friend is sad, don't say "that doesn't matter." Instead, say "I understand. How can I help?" This kind of support makes a big difference.
Person: That makes sense. Anything else?
You: Yes — trust your friend with your secrets, and be loyal. A good friend is a friend through thick and thin!
