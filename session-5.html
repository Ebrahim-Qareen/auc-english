Listening 1: Old & New Friends Online

Speaker talks about reconnecting on social media

📝 Show Subtitle / Transcript

Last month, I created an account on a social media platform. I wanted to see what my old friends were doing and maybe make some new friends too.

One day, while I was browsing my feed, I saw a picture of my best friend from primary school. Her name is Emily. We hadn't talked for years, so I sent her a message. She replied quickly and said she was so happy to hear from me. We were chatting online for hours, sharing memories and catching up on our lives.

At the same time, I joined a group for people who love books. I started talking to a girl named Mia, who loves the same books as I do. Now, we talk almost every day and share book recommendations.

Reconnecting with Emily and meeting Mia made me realize how social media can bring people together.

Social media can help you find old friends and make new ones, but it's important to use it positively!

Listening 2: A Friend in Need (Wendy & Anna)

Wendy tells a story about helping her friend Anna

📝 Show Subtitle / Transcript

Last Saturday, I was walking home from the market when I saw my friend Anna sitting on a bench. She was crying quietly, so I stopped to ask what had happened. Anna said she had lost her wallet, and she didn't have any money to take a bus home.

While we were talking, I was thinking about how I could help her. Then, I remembered that I had some extra money in my bag. I offered to pay for her bus ride, and she smiled for the first time that day.

We were waiting at the bus stop when she suddenly saw someone holding her wallet! The man was walking toward us, looking confused. He said he had found the wallet on the street and was trying to return it. Anna thanked him, and we both felt happy.

In the end, Anna got her wallet back, and she said she was grateful that I had stayed with her during that difficult moment.

Sometimes, just being there for a friend can make all the difference.

English for Social Media & Friends

📝 Show Subtitle / Transcript

Welcome to Session 5 — English for Social Media and Friends.

In this lesson, you'll learn how to talk about social media and tell stories about friends.

Key vocabulary: reconnect, browse, scroll, chat, share, platform, account, profile, post, notification, memory.

Important phrases: stay in touch, catch up, meet face-to-face, lose contact, find old friends, spend time together.

For grammar, we study past continuous and past simple together:

Past continuous = was/were + verb-ing — for background actions: "I was walking home."

Past simple = verb-ed — for sudden events: "I saw my friend."

Together: "I was walking home when I saw my friend." The past continuous sets the scene, the past simple interrupts it.

More examples: "She was studying when her phone rang." "We were chatting when the bus arrived."

📝 Step 3: Grammar — Past Continuous + Past Simple

What is the Past Continuous?

It describes a longer action happening in the background when a shorter action interrupted it.

Form: was/were + verb(-ing)

✅ Positive❌ Negative
I / He / She / Itwas + verb-ingwas not (wasn't) + verb-ing
We / You / Theywere + verb-ingwere not (weren't) + verb-ing

Past Continuous + Past Simple Together

Use when to connect two actions: the longer one (past continuous) and the shorter one (past simple).

I was talking to my friend when the phone rang.

⬅ longer action (background)     ➡ shorter action (interrupted)

Past Continuous                            Past Simple

Think of it like a movie: The past continuous is the background scene (camera showing what was happening). The past simple is the sudden event (the action!).

Examples with Friends

I was reading a book my friend gave me when the doorbell rang.

My best friend was showing me her outfit when her dog ran into the room.

I was writing a thank-you note when my pen ran out of ink.

We were taking a selfie together when someone bumped into us.

We were talking about old times when we found an old photo album.

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay — Reconnecting Online

Click each role to see guiding points and an example conversation. Practice with a partner!

🔍 Role 1: The Friend Finder

You found an old friend on social media and want to reconnect.

  • Explain how you found your old friend online
  • Describe what you were doing when you saw their profile
  • Talk about a shared memory from the past
  • Use past continuous to describe the background
  • Suggest meeting face-to-face
You: Hey! You won't believe this. I was scrolling through social media last night when I found your profile!
Friend: No way! It's been so long! What were you doing on that platform?
You: I was looking for old school friends. Do you remember when we were playing football and the ball broke the window?
Friend: Haha! Of course! The teacher was so angry. That's such a good memory.
You: We should meet face-to-face sometime. I really want to catch up properly!
Friend: Definitely! Let's stay in touch and plan something.

🤔 Role 2: The Curious Friend

Your friend found someone online. Ask questions about what happened.

  • Ask what your friend was doing when they found the person
  • Ask about the old friend (who, where, when)
  • Ask what they were talking about
  • Ask if they plan to meet face-to-face
  • Use question forms with past continuous
You: So tell me! What were you doing when you found her?
Friend: I was browsing a group for book lovers when I saw her comment.
You: That's amazing! What were you talking about?
Friend: We were sharing memories from primary school. She sent me old photos!
You: Are you going to meet face-to-face?
Friend: Yes! We're planning to meet next weekend. I'm so excited to catch up with her!

🤝 Role 3: The Helpful Friend

Tell a story about a time you helped a friend in trouble.

  • Describe what you were doing when you saw your friend needed help
  • Explain the problem your friend had
  • Describe what happened while you were helping
  • Tell how the story ended
  • Use past continuous for the background, past simple for events
You: Last week, I was walking to class when I saw my friend Ahmed sitting alone. He looked upset.
Friend: What happened?
You: He was studying for an exam but he didn't understand the grammar. While I was explaining the lesson, he started to smile.
Friend: That's nice! Did he pass?
You: Yes! He got 85%. He was so happy he bought me lunch. A friend in need is a friend indeed!

📖 Role 4: The Storyteller

Narrate a friendship story using a picture (real or imagined).

  • Set the scene: where and when was it happening?
  • Describe what people were doing in the background
  • Tell what suddenly happened (the main event)
  • Explain the lesson or feeling from the story
  • Use at least 3 past continuous + 3 past simple verbs
Narrator: It was a sunny afternoon. Some friends were sitting in the park. One of them was playing guitar while the others were laughing and telling stories.
Narrator: Suddenly, it started to rain. Everyone ran to find shelter. But one friend stayed behind because she was trying to protect the guitar from the rain.
Narrator: Her friends came back with an umbrella. They all laughed together under the rain.
Narrator: That's what real friends do — they come back for you, even in the rain.
