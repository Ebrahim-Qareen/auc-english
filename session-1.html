Welcome to Session 1 — Birthdays and the Future with "Will".

In this session, you will learn vocabulary about birthdays: words like birthday, cake, candle, balloon, present, gift, surprise, decoration, invitation, guest, celebrate, blow out.

You will also learn the months of the year: January, February, March, April, May, June, July, August, September, October, November, December.

For grammar, we will study the future with "will". We use "will" to talk about plans and decisions about the future.

Positive: I will bring a cake. She will come to the party.

Negative: I will not (won't) be late. He won't forget the present.

Questions: Will you come? Will she bring a gift?

Short answers: Yes, I will. / No, I won't.

Listen carefully and try to repeat the words you hear.

Planning a party is exciting, but you need to get ready to make it fun and special! Here are some tips to help you.

First, you need to find a place for the party. Will it be at your home, in a park, or at another special place? Next, you should send invitations to your friends and family. Make sure to tell them the date, time, and place.

Speaking of time, you need to decide what time the party will start. Is it in the afternoon or in the evening? Then, make a list of who will come. How many people are coming? This helps you plan for food and space.

Music can make the party more fun, so think about what music to play. Will you play your favorite songs or something everyone likes? You also need to plan activities to do at the party. Maybe you can play games, dance, or do something creative like drawing or singing.

If you plan these things, your party will be amazing, and everyone will have a great time!

📐 Step 3 — Grammar: Future with "Will"

When Do We Use "Will"?

1. Plans: To talk about decisions made now → "I'll buy a cake for the party."
2. Predictions: To express what you think will happen → "It will be a fun celebration!"
3. Promises: To make commitments → "I'll help you decorate the room."

Time Markers with "Will"

tomorrow, the day after tomorrow, next week/month/year, in a couple of days, in # days/weeks/months/years, on Monday/Tuesday/Wednesday...

✅ Positive: Subject + will + base verb

SubjectWillBase Verb
Iwillinvite my friends.
He / Shewilldance and sing.
Theywilleat cake.
Wewillplay fun games at the party.

More examples: I will decorate the room. / We will have a big chocolate cake. / She will love the gift. / They will arrive at 6 o'clock.

❌ Negative: Subject + will + not + base verb

SubjectWill + notBase Verb
Iwill not (won't)come.
Hewill not (won't)sleep.
Wewill not (won't)forget the cake.

won't = will not (short form). "We won't have the party at my house."

❓ Questions: Will + subject + base verb?

QuestionYes AnswerNo Answer
Will you have a party?Yes, I will.No, I won't.
Will they bring a gift?Yes, they will.No, they won't.
Will we celebrate tomorrow?Yes, we will.No, we won't.

Wh- Questions with Will

• What will you wear to the birthday party? → I will wear jeans.
• When will the birthday party start? → It will start at 7pm.
• Who will be at the birthday party? → All my friends will be there.

Transcript — The Birthday Party

Everyone loves to celebrate a birthday with friends and family members. It's part of most cultures, but not everyone celebrates the same way. There are different things that can make a cultural celebration like a birthday party special and fun. What are some of those things? Special foods, music, and games can make a cultural celebration special and fun.

Some cultures have cake to celebrate a birthday. What kind of food do you eat at your birthday party? In a lot of cultures, there is a special song or kind of music; for example, the Happy Birthday song is popular in many countries around the world. Do you listen to any special music at your birthday party? If so, what is it?

Birthday games are another really common thing to do at birthday parties in many different cultures. Playing hide-and-seek, pin the tail on the donkey, and hitting a piñata are just some of the more popular ones. What kinds of games do you play at your birthday party?

🗣 Step 5 — Speaking Challenge

🎉 Plan Your Dream Birthday Party!

Individually, imagine you are planning your BEST birthday party. Think about:

  • Where will the party be?
  • What food and drinks will be served?
  • What activities or games will be at the party?
  • Who will be invited?
  • What decorations and music will be there?

In teams of 4: Share your ideas. Ask follow-up questions using "will": "What games will you play?" / "Who will you invite?" / "What food will you serve?"

Then: From all the ideas you heard, which birthday party will be the best? Share your opinion!

🌟 The Big Dreamer

  • You are planning the most amazing birthday party ever
  • Describe everything you will do — food, games, music, decorations
  • Use "will" in every sentence: "I will invite 50 friends!"
  • Your friends ask follow-up questions

You: For my birthday, I will have a huge party at the beach!

Friend: That sounds amazing! What food will you serve?

You: I will order pizza and we will have a big chocolate cake.

Friend: Who will you invite?

You: I will invite all my friends from university. It will be the best party!

🤔 The Curious Questioner

  • Your friend is planning a party — you want to know everything!
  • Ask at least 5 questions using "will"
  • Use Wh- questions: What will...? Where will...? When will...? Who will...?
  • React to answers: "That sounds great!" / "I will come for sure!"

Planner: I'm planning my birthday party for next Saturday!

You: Oh, exciting! Where will you have it?

Planner: I will have it at my house.

You: What time will the party start?

Planner: It will start at 6 p.m.

You: Will there be music?

Planner: Yes! I will make a great playlist.

You: That sounds great! I will definitely be there!

🏆 The Party Judge

  • Listen to two friends describe their party plans
  • Compare them: "Your party will have better food, but their party will have more games"
  • Choose the best party and explain why using "will"
  • Use: "I think [name]'s party will be the best because..."

Friend A: My party will be at a restaurant. We will eat sushi!

Friend B: My party will be at home. We will play video games all night!

You: Hmm... Friend A's party will have delicious food, but Friend B's party will be more fun.

You: I think Friend B's party will be the best because we will play games and eat snacks!

😅 The "Won't" Person

  • Your friend invites you, but you have funny excuses for everything
  • Practice negative: "I won't eat cake — I'm on a diet!"
  • Use "won't" at least 5 times, then finally agree to come
  • End positively: "Okay, I will come! It will be fun!"

Friend: Will you come to my birthday party?

You: I won't come... just kidding! But I won't eat the cake.

Friend: Why not?

You: I won't eat sugar this week. But I will bring some fruit!

Friend: Will you dance at the party?

You: I won't dance... well, maybe I will! Okay, I will come and it will be amazing!

📋 Quick Cheat Sheet

🎂 Session 1 — Birthdays & "Will"

  • will + base verb for future plans: I will invite my friends.
  • won't + base verb for negative: I won't forget the cake.
  • Will + subject + base verb? for questions: Will you come?
  • Short answers: Yes, I will. / No, I won't.
  • Wh- questions: What will you bring? Where will you have it?
  • Time markers: tomorrow, next week, in a couple of days, on Saturday
  • Uses: plans, predictions, promises
  • Key vocab: birthday, celebrate, invitation, decorate, gift, candle, balloon, guest, host
  • Months: January → December (12 months)
