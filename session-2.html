📐 Step 3 — Grammar: The -ing Form & Offering Help

Rule: The -ing Form After Prepositions

The -ing form of a verb follows certain phrases that include prepositions like by, at, for, with, of, in.

PatternExample
help by + -ingI can help by bringing the drinks.
good at + -ingShe is good at organizing the games.
thank you for + -ingThank you for helping with the decorations.
help with + -ingCan I help with decorating the place?
take care of + -ingI will take care of buying the cake.
help in + -ingI will help you in inviting people.
how about + -ingHow about taking care of the food?

Offering, Accepting & Refusing Help

✅ To Offer Help:
• Can I help with...? → Can I help with the decorations?
• Would you like me to...? → Would you like me to bring the drinks?
• I'd be happy to... → I'd be happy to arrange the chairs.
• How about I take care of...? → How about I take care of the music?
👍 To Accept Help:
• Yes, please. Thank you! → Yes, please. That would be great!
• That would be great, thanks! → Thank you, I really appreciate it.
• Sure, I'd love some help with that. → Sure, I'd love some help with the food.
🚫 To Refuse Help (politely):
• No, thank you. I've got it covered. → Thanks, but I've got it covered.
• I think I can manage, but thanks anyway. → I can manage, but thanks!
• That's kind of you, but I'm fine. → That's really kind, but I'm fine.
📌 Remember: offer/refuse + to + base verb
• I offer to help you. (NOT: offer helping)
• He refuses to come. (NOT: refuses coming)
• Would you like to organize the party? (NOT: like organizing — in this context)

Reading — Planning a Party (Emma's Party)

Emma's Party

Emma will have a party at her house next weekend. She will begin by planning the menu and choosing the decorations. She will make a list of everything she will need, like food, drinks, and music.

Emma will start by preparing sandwiches and pizza for her friends. She will also bake a big chocolate cake. For drinks, she will focus on buying orange juice, soda, and water. She will decorate the living room by hanging balloons and putting up a colorful banner.

Emma will send invitations to her friends on Friday. She will ask them to come at 6 p.m., and to help by bringing their favorite games to play together. Emma hopes the party will be fun for everyone.

🗣 Step 5 — Speaking Challenge

🎉 Party Planning Conversation

In teams of 4, plan a role-play:

  • One person is the party planner
  • The others are friends offering to help
  • Use: Can I help with...? / Would you like me to...? / How about I take care of...?
  • Practice accepting AND refusing help politely
  • After 2 minutes, switch roles — new person plans a different party!

💬 Reflection Questions

After your roleplay, discuss:

  • Share one good example of offering, accepting, or refusing help
  • How did it feel to offer help? To refuse help politely?
  • Which phrases were easiest/hardest to use?

🎂 The Party Planner

  • You are planning a big birthday party for Saturday
  • You need help with decorations, food, music, and drinks
  • Accept some offers and politely refuse others
  • Use: "Yes, please! That would be great!" / "Thanks, but I've got it covered."

You: Hey everyone! I'm planning a birthday party for Saturday. There's a lot to do!

Friend: Can I help with the decorations?

You: Yes, please! That would be great. Can you bring some balloons?

Friend: Sure! Would you like me to bring streamers too?

You: That's kind of you, but I've already got streamers. Thanks anyway!

🙋 The Eager Helper

  • You want to help your friend with their party
  • Offer to help with at least 3 different things
  • Use -ing form: "I'm good at decorating / I can help by bringing..."
  • Accept polite refusals gracefully

You: I heard you're planning a party! Can I help with anything?

Planner: Well, I need someone to handle the music.

You: I'm good at choosing music! I can help by making a playlist.

Planner: That would be amazing! Can you also bring a speaker?

You: Sure! How about I take care of setting up the sound system too?

Planner: That's really kind, but I think I can manage that myself. Thanks though!

You: No problem! Let me know if you need anything else.

🍕 The Food Expert

  • You are great at cooking and baking
  • Offer to help with the food and drinks
  • Use: "I can help by preparing..." / "Thank you for letting me help with..."
  • Ask about what food the planner needs

Planner: I still need to figure out the food for the party.

You: I'd be happy to help with that! I'm good at baking.

Planner: Really? Would you like to make a cake?

You: Sure! I can help by baking a chocolate cake. And how about I bring some snacks too?

Planner: Yes, please! That would be really helpful. Thank you for offering!

You: Thank you for letting me help with the food! It'll be fun.

😊 The Polite Refuser

  • You are organized and prefer to do most things yourself
  • Politely refuse some offers while accepting others
  • Use: "That's kind of you, but..." / "I think I can manage, but thanks!"
  • Always be friendly and grateful

Friend: Would you like me to bring the drinks?

You: That's kind of you, but I've already bought everything. Thanks anyway!

Friend: How about I help with setting up the chairs?

You: I think I can manage, but thanks for asking!

Friend: Can I at least help with the music playlist?

You: Actually, yes please! I'm not very good at choosing music. That would be great!
